# mathemame 📜

*The settled state in which a long discipline has finally yielded — and what it yields is held not as achievement but as gift received.*

mathemame is two true roots welded into one word: the Greek **mathēma** (μάθημα), "that-which-is-learned," from *manthanō*, to learn — whose perfect aspect *memathēka* means "I am in the state of having-learned" — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So mathemame is learned-content held as a cosmic decree: the yield of long sustained practice, registered as gift rather than personal trophy. It is the **first inhabitant and keystone** of the cathedral's canon/mathema/ domain — the word that domain was opened to house — and the twelfth member of the *me*-family.

mathemame names an *abiding state*, not a moment: five features must hold together — discipline-mediated, received-not-produced, persisting, tradition-implicated, and worship-grade. It is the mature mathematician through whom a field's questions now flow, the monk owned by the psalms, the luthier whose hands know the craft before the mind directs them. Its light is that it honors the long labor while naming the fruit as gift; its shadow is the false humility that dodges mastery's duties, the empty tenure that counterfeits the yield, and the tradition-chain bent into a gatekeeper's cage. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.35/10**.
